The brief

An artisanal leather atelier wanted to grow into the B2B fashion market, but its website wasn't speaking the right language.

Chocholackova is an independent artisanal leather atelier based in Amsterdam. The brief was to research the brand's potential to expand into the B2B leather fashion market and to develop a positioning that communicated its services clearly to that new audience through its website.

02 Approach

01

Persona development

Built B2B consumer personas using secondary research from interviews, personal websites, and social media profiles to understand who the atelier needed to reach.

02

Usability testing

Designed a two part test: a scenario based task where participants searched for a leather designer through Google, followed by content focused usability testing of the website itself.

03

SEO and content clarity

Reviewed how findable and understandable the website's services were, and identified where terminology and structure were creating friction for B2B visitors.

03 Execution

Branding manual produced alongside the research

B2B consumer persona built from secondary research

Usability test brief given to participants

The atelier's website tested for content clarity

Live usability test combining search and content tasks

What the work delivered

B2B consumer personas that clarified who the atelier needed to speak to in the leather fashion market

A two phase usability test combining real search behaviour with content level website testing

Specific recommendations on terminology, positioning, and service clarity based directly on participant behaviour

A clearer foundation for the atelier's B2B facing content and search visibility

04 Key takeaway

Good service doesn't sell itself if the words around it don't make sense to the person looking for it. Watching real people search and read the site, rather than assuming what they'd understand, was what made the recommendations land.
