The brief

During my Master's at Amsterdam Fashion Institute, I was challenged to build a circular fashion startup from the ground up.

COCLO is a styling mobile application built for a more sustainable and active wardrobe, letting people catalog, style, and resell their clothes in one place. Over two years I took the project from a theoretical concept to a working prototype, addressing real social and environmental issues within the fashion industry.

02 Approach

01

Consumer research

Studied the psychology of consumer behaviour around fashion and wardrobes, analysing 204 posts in the fashion closet sub-Reddit and 111 Pinterest fashion closet boards to understand existing community behaviour.

02

Concept development

Explored swapping, dry cleaning, repurposing, alterations, styling, and social platforms as potential service directions, in collaboration with the London College of Fashion MA programme.

03

Prototyping and testing

Sketched, prototyped, and ran A/B tests, UX and UI testing, focus groups, interviews, and surveys to refine the product into a functioning Figma prototype.

04 Key takeaway

Sustainability in fashion doesn't need to feel like a sacrifice. COCLO worked because it gave people a way to enjoy their existing wardrobe more, not just a reason to consume less.
